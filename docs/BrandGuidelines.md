Brand IdentifiersLogomark: An iconic, stylized tulip- or kernel-shaped symbol outlined in gold. Used as a compact identifier in tight visual spaces such as social media avatars, favicons, or in contexts where brand recognition is already well-established.  Wordmark (Logotype): The custom typographic form of the brand name "GOURMET POPCORNICA". It serves as the primary brand identifier, engineered for maximum clarity and impact across all brand communications.  Logo Lockups: The standard layout combines the wordmark and the logomark into a unified composition. This default lockup is preferred for immediate brand recognition.  2. Size & Spatial RulesMinimum Dimensions: To preserve legibility, reproduction must never drop below the following sizes:  Logomark: Print $\ge$ 10mm | Digital $\ge$ 50px   Logotype: Print $\ge$ 50mm | Digital $\ge$ 150px   Full Logo Lockup: Print $\ge$ 50mm | Digital $\ge$ 150px   Clearspace ($X$-Value): Clearspace defines a strict protected buffer surrounding the entire logo asset. This baseline unit ($X$) is proportional to the logo dimensions, and this boundary must remain completely free of any intersecting text, graphics, or edge margins.  5. Logo Misuse ProhibitionsTo preserve brand integrity, the logo must never be modified or distorted in any way. Strictly avoid:  Stretching or altering the aspect ratio.  Rotating or shearing the orientation.  Adding restrictive background containers or shapes around the logo lockup.  Flipping the asset horizontally or vertically. Background and Text Pairing RulesOn Dark Backgrounds (Midnight Navy / Deep Cobalt): Headlines must use Kernel Cream (#FDF3E3); Subheads must use Maize Glow (#F5C96E). Body text sits directly on the background for warmth and high contrast.  On Light Backgrounds (Kernel Cream / Warm Sand): Headlines must use Midnight Navy (#0C1433). Subheads must use Harvest Bronze (#A07825). Body text must be Midnight Navy rendered at either 65% opacity or full value.  On Accent Blocks (Buttered Gold): Always pair with Midnight Navy (#0C1433) text. Never use white text on gold background as it fails standard accessibility contrast tests.  On Highlight Tags (Maize Glow): Always pair with Navy text (#0C1433). Best utilized strictly for small tags, chips, or callout pills; do not use as a background across wide surface areas.
